The Shape of Modern Games

An Exploratory Data Analysis of Steam Releases (2021–2025)

This website presents a concise overview of an exploratory data analysis of Steam game releases from 2021 to 2025. The analysis focuses on pricing patterns, publication frequency, genre composition, play modes, and player recommendations in order to identify structural trends within the contemporary PC game market.

Hover over each graph to reveal a short interpretation.

Pricing Overview

The majority of games released on Steam during this period are priced below €10, resulting in a low average price for paid titles. While the average price of games peaks around 2023, the limited timeframe and potential external influences mean that no strong long-term pricing trend can be reliably inferred from this data alone.

Release Trends

The number of games released on Steam increases steadily from 2021 to 2025, indicating a clear upward trend in publication frequency. Within individual years, releases are relatively evenly distributed but show noticeable peaks in autumn months.

Play Modes: Single vs. Multiplayer

Single-player games make up the majority of Steam releases across all observed years. Multiplayer titles are less common but increase steadily over time, indicating growth in both categories rather than a shift from one to the other.

Genre Landscape

Genre co-occurrence analysis reveals a dominant central cluster of highly frequent and closely related genres, particularly Indie, Action, and Adventure. More specialized genres appear more isolated, reflecting narrower overlap and lower overall frequency within the Steam catalog.

Popular Games

Popularity is approximated using the number of user recommendations, producing a ranked list of the most visible games released during the observed period. Insights & Limitations

These findings describe patterns observed in a recent subset of the Steam catalog and should be interpreted as exploratory rather than predictive. Several structural limitations apply:

Key Caveats

  • Temporal scope: The dataset covers releases from 2021–2025 only, limiting insight into longer-term historical trends.
  • Popularity proxy: Recommendation counts are used as an indicator of popularity, but do not directly measure revenue, sales volume, or long-term engagement.
  • External influences: Economic conditions, platform policies, marketing efforts, and post-release updates may affect outcomes independently of price or genre.
  • Catalog coverage: Only titles present in the dataset are analyzed; delisted, region-restricted, or missing entries are not represented.
